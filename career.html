How It All Started

It was once a little known fact that Ringo's entire drumming career with the Beatles was the product of an altercation with the Beatles original drummer, Pete Best.

In 1962, the Beatles were setting up to play a concert at a venue owned by Ringo's parents. Competitive in nature, Ringo used his family connection to get back stage and challenge Pete Best to a "drum off." Unaware that Pete was inebriated on this particular night, Ringo had the bright idea to introduce himself by knocking a drumstick out of Pete's hand. This began a physical fight in which Ringo not only broke Pete's dominant arm, but did so in front of Pete's girlfriend, causing Pete a level of embarrassment so severe that he quit the band and left town immediately.

Because the Beatles were now without a drummer for the night, they had no choice but to allow young Ringo a chance to prove his skills and fill in a their drummer for the night. Ringo did so well, he was asked to become the new drummer.

Ringo was born a lefty, but in the era he grew up in, almost all items were made solely for people with a dominant right hand. Having to adapt to right-handed equipment and instruments is a part of what made his drumming style so unique. His style was so unique, in fact, that Ringo felt he was the real star of the band. "Without my beat, the ladies don't move thier feet.", declares Ringo between drags of his cigarette.

How Constant Jealousy Kept The Band From Including Ringo During Awards Ceremonies

The Beatles only won 4 Grammys together before the group broke up.

  • Best New Artist, 1964
  • Best Performance by a Vocal Group, 1964 (“A Hard Day’s Night”)
  • Best Contemporary Album, 1967 (Sergeant Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band)
  • Album of the Year, 1967 (Sergeant Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band)
